Progressive Confirmation and Accessibility Design Philosophy

ICI implements an "unapologetically naggy" confirmation methodology designed to ensure user safety and accessibility across all ability levels. This approach includes:

  • Progressive Explanation: Actions are explained in multiple ways with increasing detail and clarity.
  • Multi-Modal Confirmations: Confirmations use various methods (visual, textual, interactive) to accommodate different accessibility needs.
  • Graceful Repetition: The system will ask multiple times in different ways to ensure understanding, particularly for destructive or significant actions.
  • Contextual Warnings: Clear, contextual explanations of consequences before actions are taken.
  • Accessibility-First: All confirmations are designed to work with screen readers, keyboard navigation, and other assistive technologies.

This design philosophy prioritizes user agency and informed consent over convenience, ensuring that individuals with cognitive disabilities maintain full control and understanding of their interactions with the system. The "naggy" approach is intentional and considered a feature, not a bug.

The use of these tools and practices is intended to:
